Product Variations

There are over 100 distinct styles of beer (over 73 ales, over 25 lagers, and a few hybrid styles), but Holsten beers is unique.

The majority of beers are classified as lagers or ales. Lagers are fermented with yeast that grows at the bottom of the beer mixture, while ales are fermented with yeast that grows at the top. Apart from the yeast used to make lagers and ales, there are yeasts that ferment spontaneously, producing wild or sour beers.

The yeast used in the production of ale ferments throughout the beer and settles to the top. It is more tolerant of alcohol and ferments at higher temperatures than the yeast used to make lager. Top fermenting ales include IPAs, stouts, and wheat beers.


Add To Cart

Lager yeast is more delicate than ale yeast, and it settles to the bottom of the liquid vessel following fermentation. It requires a slower rate of fermentation and lower temperatures than the yeast used in ale production, and it has a lower tolerance for alcohol. Bottom fermenting lagers include pilsners, bocks, and Okerberfests.

Lambics and sour beers are produced using a technique known as spontaneous fermentation. When beer is exposed to wild bacteria and yeast, this type of fermentation occurs. Although these beers originated in Belgium, brewers worldwide have discovered ways to manipulate this process to create their own sour, funky-tasting beers.
